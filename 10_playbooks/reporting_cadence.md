# Reporting cadence playbook

How the Marketing Data Analyst & CRM Manager runs the reporting OS week to week. Definitions live in [funnel_definitions.md](../01_reporting_system/funnel_definitions.md).

## Cadence

| Cadence | Timebox | Artifact | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Daily | 30–45 min | [daily_report.md](../01_reporting_system/daily_report.md) | Volume, outliers, same-day hygiene |
| Weekly | ~half day | [weekly_report.md](../01_reporting_system/weekly_report.md) | Decision pack: 3 findings max |
| Monthly | deeper pull | [monthly_report.md](../01_reporting_system/monthly_report.md) | Trends, concentration, stop/continue/double |

## Efficiency rules
1. If a report does not change budget, territory, message, or CRM process, stop producing it.
2. Automate pulls; spend human time on interpretation and CRM exceptions.
3. Default deliverable: short narrative + one table + one chart.
4. Own data quality as a product: weekly defect list.
5. One conversation with a liaison beats three vanity dashboards when reading field activity.

## Week-one freeze
- Lock funnel and taxonomy with leadership.
- Freeze unless leadership changes definitions.
- Disputed definitions go to the stakeholder decision map as open decisions, not silent rewrites inside reports.
