# AI playbook

Use AI to accelerate output after data hygiene is real. Do not use AI to paper over missing attribution or dirty stages. Clean data first; content second.

Applies across the growth-readiness system: reporting, CRM hygiene assist, digital execution, and referral-relationship collateral.

## Allowed uses

### Reporting
- Draft the weekly decision-pack narrative from a filled scorecard (3 findings max)
- Turn monthly trends into a one-page leadership brief
- Flag anomalies in exports (volume spikes, CPL vs cost-per-SOC divergence, coverage drops)

### CRM and taxonomy
- Propose cleanup queues from defect lists (missing source, missing campaign, overdue follow-up)
- Normalize campaign naming suggestions to the master taxonomy
- Draft picklist rationalization options for owner review (never auto-apply)

### Referral collateral (NY home healthcare)
- Facility / hospital one-pagers for liaison leave-behinds
- Follow-up email variants for discharge planners, CMs, MD offices, MLTC contacts
- Short AI-generated video concepts for relationship development (hooks, outline, CTA)
- Event or in-service talking points

### Campaign support
- Ad copy and landing variants that map to named campaigns in CRM
- Geo or audience angle drafts for borough/county catchments

## Hard rules
1. No PHI in prompts, pastes, or generated collateral drafts destined for broad tools.
2. No inventing metrics. AI drafts narrative from provided numbers only.
3. No strategy theater. Outputs must be usable by a liaison or leadership in one pass.
4. Hygiene before content. If unknown-source defect rate is high, prioritize cleanup prompts.
5. Human owns recommendations that move budget, territory, or CRM policy.

## Sequence
1. Confirm taxonomy and funnel freeze
2. Run daily/weekly hygiene
3. Fill scorecards with real extracts
4. Then use AI for briefs, anomaly notes, and collateral
